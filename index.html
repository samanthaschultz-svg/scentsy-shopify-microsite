A note for the Scentsy leadership team

The case for a simpler commerce foundation — and why now is the right moment to revisit it.

We've spent time over the last year exploring what it would look like for Scentsy to move to Shopify. This is a short refresh of where that thinking lands today, written for a wider group at Scentsy who weren't part of those earlier conversations.

From Samantha Schultz, Lead Account Executive — Shopify Enterprise

A different question than a year ago.

A year ago, the question was whether Shopify could match what Scentsy has built. The answer to that one is settled — it can, with the right partner layer on top. The harder question was always about timing and resourcing: was a replatform worth pulling engineers off of growth and AI work?

Heading into the back half of 2026, that calculus has shifted. With a leaner team and a roadmap focused on AI, agentic commerce, and the consultant experience, the central question becomes: where should your engineering investment actually go?

The pitch is no longer "rebuild commerce on a new platform." It's: stop owning the parts of commerce that are now industry-standard, and put that capacity into the things only Scentsy can build.

Why now

Four shifts that change the math compared to even six months ago.

01

The cost of maintaining your own commerce stack is going up

Checkout, payments, fraud, international localization, social commerce, and now agentic commerce surfaces are all moving fast. Keeping pace requires sustained engineering investment in areas where you don't differentiate. A leaner team makes that math harder to justify every quarter.

02

Agentic commerce is becoming a distribution channel

AI assistants and agentic shopping experiences are starting to drive real volume. Most of the early integrations — Perplexity, OpenAI, Shop App — sit on top of Shopify. Being on the platform isn't a differentiator anymore; being off of it is increasingly a disadvantage.

03

The direct-selling pattern on Shopify is no longer experimental

Party-plan, consultant attribution, host rewards, subscriptions, multi-currency, cross-border consultant flows — these are well-trodden patterns now, with implementation partners who specialize in direct selling. The risk profile of a migration is materially lower than it was even a year ago.

04

Refocusing engineering on what makes Scentsy, Scentsy

The consultant experience, the AI-powered selling tools, the perennial-catalog model — these are where your moat is. Migrating commerce frees the team to spend its time there instead of maintaining infrastructure that the rest of the industry has already commoditized.

What changes, what stays

A frame for the conversation. The point is consolidation, not replacement.

Stays exactly where it is

  • Consultant workstation — your back office and party-plan tooling
  • Commissions engine — payouts, ranks, downline logic
  • PIM — and the seasonal / perennial catalog workflow
  • ERP — master data, financial system of record
  • Content stack — already integrates with Shopify
  • Customer service tooling — including the proxy-login flow
  • Consultant CRM app — integrates via APIs

Shopify takes over

  • Storefronts — global, multi-currency, multi-language
  • Checkout & Shop Pay — the conversion lift driving the case
  • Payments — orchestration, processing, fraud in one
  • Subscriptions — your recurring fragrance program
  • Social commerce — TikTok, Instagram, Meta, YouTube
  • Agentic surfaces — Shop App, Perplexity, OpenAI
  • Marketing engine — Campaigns, Audiences, Collective

A partner builds the bridge

  • Direct-selling layer — consultant attribution, party orders, host rewards
  • Cross-border consultant flows — agreements, market activation
  • Custom user types — customers, consultants, hosts, affiliates
  • Workstation integration — embedded back-office widgets
  • Group / phone / party order patterns — multi-card, multi-cart, multi-ship
  • Reporting refresh — pruned and modernized at launch

You wouldn't be the first to make this move.

Scentsy's direct-selling peers are already operating on Shopify, with the consultant, party-plan, subscription, and global rollout patterns running in production today. The playbook exists.

For a brand at Scentsy's scale, the Shopify Enterprise relationship also includes direct access to product and executive teams, and earlier visibility into growth features as they ship.

A small next step.

No deck, no pitch. A 30-minute conversation with the right people from your side and ours, to figure out whether the work we did last year still lines up with where Scentsy is heading now. If it doesn't, that's a useful answer too.
